Three Core Problems

1. Fragmented Site Architecture

Archive subdomain had 2.5x more traffic than the main site. Redirect chains wasting crawl budget. Support portal (Zendesk) disconnected from main domain SEO. No internal linking strategy — three subdomains operating as separate islands.

2. Content Not Converting

Core commercial keywords stuck on page 2 (#15–19). Solution pages had rankings but 0.02% CTR. Content in the pipeline but not reaching production. Zero comparison or competitor-targeted content to capture bottom-of-funnel buyers.

3. Invisible to AI

Ranked #5 of 8 competitors in AI citations (13.7% visibility). #1 competitor had 2.2x higher visibility. Zero presence in top-of-funnel AI queries. No structured data or schema strategy for LLM discoverability.

What We Did — Month by Month

Month 1 (December 2025) — Foundation & Technical

Technical SEO: Initiated archive-to-main-domain migration (301 redirects) to consolidate 2.5x traffic leak. Fixed redirect chains wasting crawl budget. Deployed XML sitemap for new pages. Zendesk support subdomain robots.txt fix. Solutions hub page redesign.

Content production: Published 9 customer case study pages + 4 competitor switcher articles (pricing analysis, comparison, migration guide, hidden fees). All indexed within weeks.

Competitive analysis: Mapped 85 competitor content URLs with traffic estimates. Identified 6 content gap categories. Built keyword tracking for 12 core commercial keywords. Developed 4-month traffic projection (+6,000 monthly visits).

Month 2 (January 2026) — Content Strategy & First Wins

Content production: Published first wave of keyword-targeted blog articles (competitor comparison content, industry education). Launched competitor pricing analysis article (became top AI-referred landing page).

AEO foundation: Set up AI visibility tracking (WorkDuo). Established baseline across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overview. Built comparison content specifically designed for LLM citation.

Measurement: Designed full measurement architecture (GA4 event taxonomy, attribution model, cross-platform data pipeline). Set up AI traffic tracking in GA4.

Month 3 (February 2026) — Acceleration

Content velocity: Continued blog expansion with education articles and product cluster content. Achieved 57% of all site clicks from blog (up from 34%).

Off-page AEO: Launched outreach email campaign (~278 emails). Partnership outreach for citation building. Comparison content development continued.

Results validation: AI visibility surged +9.2pp in a single month. Core keyword coverage jumped from 3 to 6 keywords in top 10. First AI-referred key events recorded in GA4.

What Are Core Keywords?

Core keywords are the most commercially relevant product queries in a client's market — the search terms that signal a buyer actively evaluating solutions. We track these separately because they give direct insight into lower-funnel positioning: where you rank for the queries that actually drive demos, trials, and purchase decisions.

For Client A, we track 12 core keywords (e.g., "[product category] software", "best [product category] platform"). These are distinct from educational or informational keywords — a blog ranking for "how to do X" drives awareness, but a core keyword ranking for "[product category] software" drives pipeline. Moving from 1 to 6 core keywords in the top 10 means 6x more visibility on the queries where purchase intent is highest.

AI-Referred Traffic — The Real Numbers

GA4 tracks direct AI referrals (chatgpt.com, perplexity.ai, etc.), but this significantly underestimates true AI-influenced traffic. Users who discover the brand through AI often return via direct URL or branded search, appearing as "direct" or "organic" in analytics.

Client A's internal estimate: true AI-referred traffic is 5–7x what GA4 attributes. Based on:

  • CEO receiving direct outreach from prospects who mentioned "ChatGPT recommended you"
  • Branded search uplift correlating with AI visibility increases
  • Demo request form data mentioning AI platforms as discovery source
  • Industry research showing 60-80% of AI-influenced visits are misattributed in GA4

Estimated true AI-referred sessions: ~1,130/month (Feb 2026), with 14 key conversion events — first measurable pipeline from AI channels.

What Made It Work

Technical Foundation First

Fixing the CMS bug (suppressed 60+ posts) before investing in new content meant everything we published reached its full potential immediately. One article was already ranking #4 for a 5,400 SV keyword without a title tag — imagine what happens when the basics are right.

Comparison Content = AI Magnet

Competitor comparison articles became the #1 AI-referred landing pages. LLMs love structured, balanced comparisons. This single content type drove disproportionate AI visibility gains.

AI Moves Faster Than Google

AI visibility jumped +9.2pp in one month while Google organic grew steadily. Different engines, different timelines. The 6-month AI visibility KPI was achieved in month 3. AEO delivers faster wins than traditional SEO alone.

Client Collaboration Is the Multiplier

Weekly syncs with the client team informed content direction. When they shared an industry shakeup (competitor pricing change), we produced 5 targeted articles in 48 hours. One of those generated the most qualified leads of the quarter — not from volume, but from relevance and timing.

Schema Deployment = Competitive Moat

Client A now has the strongest structured data implementation in their competitive set. Competitors either have none or broken implementations. This is a durable advantage for both Google featured snippets and AI citations.

ICP Alignment Over Volume

We initially projected 10x growth. After understanding the client's ICP, we recalibrated to 3-5x with higher quality. Commercial-intent keyword rankings grew faster than informational ones, driving demo requests, not just page views.
